# Partner analytics engine

Arcademy’s partner analytics helps you answer one question: **Are learners actually getting value from your courses, and where should you improve next?**

When **Analytics V2** is enabled for your project, you get a multi-tab workspace in the Partner console. The tabs match what you see in the analytics navigation bar:

![Analytics dashboard overview](/partner-docs/analytics/analytics-dashboard-overview.png)

  <Card title="Analytics settings" href="/partner-console">

  </Card>

## Two analytics experiences

| Experience | When you see it | What you get |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Analytics V2** | Enabled in Analytics settings (Owner+) | Seven tabs: Overview, Courses, Concepts, Assessments, Readiness, Certifications, Recommendations |
| **Partner Analytics Plus** (classic) | V2 flag is off | Single-page report with summary cards, funnel, trends, and course drill-down |

Most partners should use **Analytics V2**. The classic report remains available if your team has not switched yet.

## Before you read each tab

Every analytics page shares the same **controls at the top**:

### Period (date range)

Metrics only include activity inside the window you select:

- **7 days** — recent pulse check
- **30 days** — default; good for monthly reviews
- **90 days** — seasonal or campaign-length view
- **All time** — full history since launch

If cohorts or recommendations look empty, try a wider period.

### Compare to (historical comparison)

When enabled, KPI cards show **% change vs the prior matching window** (for example, this 30 days vs the previous 30 days). “No prior period” means there is not enough earlier data yet.

Pick a comparison length that matches your Period so the delta is meaningful.

### Snapshot status

Analytics numbers are built from **saved snapshots** so pages load quickly. The status chip tells you:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Up to date** | Numbers match the latest snapshot for this period |
| **Needs refresh** | Content or activity changed since the last build |
| **Building / Queued** | Recalculating — older numbers may show briefly |
| **Build failed** | Refresh to retry, or contact Arcademy staff if it persists |

**Owners** and **staff** can trigger a manual refresh. **Analysts** always see the latest available snapshot.

### Export

Each tab can export its view (Markdown, CSV, or a full analytics pack depending on scope). Use export for stakeholder reports or offline review.

### Privacy

Partner analytics is **aggregate only**. You never see individual learner identities, wallet addresses, or referral attribution.

## Tab 1: Overview

**Purpose:** Executive health check — “How are we doing this period?”

Start here every review cycle.

### Executive summary KPIs

These six cards are your headline numbers:

| KPI | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| **Health score** | 0–100 blend of completion rate, quiz pass rate, and start conversion for this period. Higher usually means more learners finish and pass. Not a platform-wide grade. |
| **Learners started** | Distinct learners who started at least one published course in this period |
| **Completion rate** | Share of starters who finished required lessons **and** passed the final quiz. Not the same as earning a badge in every edge case, but closely related |
| **Badges awarded** | Course badges granted in this period. Progress signals on Arcademy — separate from certifications |
| **Quiz pass rate** | Among learners who attempted a final quiz, the share who passed at least once |
| **Start conversion** | Course starts ÷ course page views. Shows how many visitors who open a course actually begin it |

Each KPI may show a green ↑ or red ↓ delta when Compare to is active.

### Top recommendations preview

The Overview shows your **top three** actionable recommendations with links to the full Recommendations tab and the evidence behind each alert.

### Learning funnel

![Learning funnel](/partner-docs/analytics/learning-funnel.svg)

The funnel counts **tracked events in order**. The percentage on each step is **this step’s events ÷ the previous step’s events** — not unique people.

Typical stages:

1. Course page views
2. Course starts
3. Lesson completions
4. Quiz passed / badge earned

A large drop between steps tells you where to investigate. The Recommendations tab may flag funnel drops that fall below your configured threshold.

### Cohorts

Learners grouped by the **week they first started** a course. Completers in cohorts are learners who earned a course badge — useful as a retention signal, but not identical to the Completion rate KPI above.

### Learning behaviour

Total engagement event counts in this period: lesson views, lesson completions, quiz starts, hints used, glossary opens, search queries. These are **counts of actions**, not unique learners.

### Weekly trends

Chart of key metrics over time within the selected period.

### Courses preview

Top five courses by activity with links to the full Courses tab and per-course detail pages.

### Staff notes

Optional notes from Arcademy staff about your analytics context (if present).

## Tab 2: Courses

**Purpose:** Per-course performance — which courses attract learners, where they drop off, and how quizzes behave.

### Courses table

Each published course shows:

| Column | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Starts** | Distinct learners who started this course in the period |
| **Completions** | Learners who finished required lessons and passed the final quiz |
| **Completion %** | Completions ÷ starts |
| **Badges** | Badges awarded for this course in the period |
| **Quiz pass %** | Share of quiz attempters who passed at least once |

Click a course row for the **course detail** page.

### Course detail (drill-down)

The detail view goes deeper on one course:

- **Lesson funnel** — completion rate step-by-step through lessons; highlights the lesson with the largest drop vs the previous lesson
- **Quiz diagnostics** — per-question miss rates, discrimination proxy, average time
- **Attempts before pass** — histogram of how many tries learners needed
- **Within-2-attempt pass rate** — share who passed on attempt 1 or 2 (low values often mean the quiz is too hard or unclear)
- **Average quiz score** and **average attempts to pass**
- **Badge verification views** — times someone opened a public badge verification page

Use course detail when a specific course underperforms while others look healthy.

## Tab 3: Concepts

**Purpose:** Understand **what learners know** at the topic level — not just whether they finished a course.

### What is a concept?

A **concept** is a topic or skill you define for your product (for example, “Private transactions”, “Key management”, “SDK setup”). Concepts are **your taxonomy** — the vocabulary you use to describe what learners should master.

![Concepts workflow](/partner-docs/analytics/analytics-concepts-flow.png)

Concepts power three analytics areas:

1. **Concepts tab** — mastery and knowledge gaps
2. **Assessments tab** — which questions test which concepts
3. **Readiness tab** — concept mastery as one readiness component

**Untagged content is invisible to concept analytics.** A lesson or quiz question with no concept tags does not contribute to mastery or gap scores.

### Concept importance levels

When you create a concept in Settings, you assign importance:

| Level | Weight in gap scoring | When to use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Critical** | 1.5× | Must-know for safe or successful product use |
| **Core** | 1.0× | Standard curriculum topics |
| **Supporting** | 0.6× | Nice-to-have or introductory context |

Critical concepts that score poorly surface first in **Knowledge gaps** and can trigger Recommendations.

### Concept coverage banner

At the top of the Concepts tab, coverage shows what share of your **published lessons and quiz questions** have at least one concept tag.

Example: “72% coverage — 18/25 lessons and 45/60 questions tagged.”

Aim for high coverage before trusting mastery numbers. Link from the banner goes to Analytics settings to manage concepts and tagging.

### Knowledge gaps

Ranked list of concepts with the highest **gap score**:

```
gap score = importance weight × (1 − mastery %)
```

Higher gap = more urgent. Fix content, add practice, or revise assessments for concepts at the top of this list.

### Concept mastery table

| Column | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Importance** | Critical / core / supporting |
| **Tagged lessons / questions** | How much content maps to this concept |
| **Attempts** | Quiz answers on tagged questions in this period |
| **Mastery %** | Weighted correct rate (0–100) on tagged questions |
| **Gap score** | Urgency ranking |

Click a concept for the **concept drill-down**: linked lessons, linked questions, and per-question miss rates.

### Tagging content

Concept tags are applied when staff or partners edit **lessons** and **quiz questions** in the course editor. Each tag can carry a weight (how strongly that content represents the concept).

**Manager+** roles can manage the concept list in Settings. Tagging happens in content authoring, not on the Concepts tab itself.

### Data requirement

Concept mastery requires normalized quiz attempt data. If your project is still backfilling historical attempts, the Concepts tab may show a setup gate until processing completes.

## Tab 4: Assessments

**Purpose:** Quiz question quality — find confusing items, high miss rates, and untagged questions.

### Concept coverage (assessments)

Same coverage idea as Concepts: questions without concept tags limit your ability to connect misses to topics.

### Untagged question warning

Lists questions with no concept tags when coverage is incomplete. Tag them so misses roll up into Concepts and Readiness.

### Question intelligence table

| Column | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | Question text (truncated) |
| **Quiz / course** | Where the question lives |
| **Attempts** | Answered attempts in the period |
| **Miss rate** | Share of attempts that were incorrect |
| **Avg time** | Average time spent on this question per attempt |
| **Discrimination** | How well the question separates strong vs weak quiz performance. Higher is usually better. Near zero or negative may mean a confusing or mis-keyed item |
| **Concepts** | Linked concept names |

Sorted by miss rate by default — the hardest questions float to the top.

Click a question for **question drill-down** with full prompt, linked concepts, and attempt breakdown.

## Tab 5: Readiness

**Purpose:** A single **readiness score** that blends multiple learning signals — useful when “finished the course” is not enough to mean “ready to build.”

![Readiness model](/partner-docs/analytics/readiness-model.svg)

### Default model: Learning Readiness

New projects seed a default model with four components at **25% weight each**:

| Component | What it measures |
| --- | --- |
| **Course completion** | Share of starters who complete courses |
| **Quiz performance** | Aggregate quiz pass / score performance |
| **Concept mastery** | Mastery on critical and core concepts (requires tagging) |
| **Required path completion** | Progress through required learning paths |

**Partner conversion events** (for example, “visited docs” or “joined Discord”) appear as a configurable component but are **deferred** — not tracked in V1 analytics.

### What you see on the page

For each readiness model:

- **Average score** — weighted mix, 0–100
- **Level** — label from configured tiers (default: Exploring → Building → Ready)
- **Ready threshold** — minimum score to count as “ready” (default **70**)
- **Component breakdown** — each component’s score, weight, and status

### Component statuses

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **OK** | Component is configured and contributing real data |
| **Neutral** | Not fully set up — scores a neutral 50 so missing setup does not break the model |
| **Setup needed** | Action required (for example, add concepts, tag questions, define paths). Follow the setup link |

Owners can rename the model, change weights, and adjust the ready threshold in **Analytics settings → Readiness model**. Weights should sum to **100%**.

## Tab 6: Certifications

**Purpose:** Track **formal credentials** you define — separate from Arcademy course badges.

![Badges vs certifications](/partner-docs/analytics/badges-vs-certifications.svg)

| | Badge | Certification |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Who defines it** | Arcademy course completion rules | You, in Analytics settings |
| **When it’s earned** | Automatically on course completion | When certification requirements are met |
| **Where it appears** | Overview, Courses, learner profile | Certifications tab |
| **Promoted automatically?** | No — never auto-becomes a certification | — |

### Credential funnel

Steps such as:

1. Published certifications configured
2. Learners started (baseline)
3. Certifications awarded
4. Certificate views

Each step shows a count and conversion % from the previous step.

### Attainment summary

- **Awards in period** — certifications granted in the selected date range
- **Certificate views** — times verification pages were opened

### Per-certification table

| Column | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Status** | Draft or published |
| **Requirements** | Number of rules (for example, complete Course X) |
| **Awards (period)** | Granted in this period |
| **All-time** | Total awards ever |
| **Attainment %** | Awards ÷ learners who started — how often starters earn this credential |

Create certifications in **Analytics settings → Certifications**. Managers can create; Owners control full lifecycle.

## Tab 7: Recommendations

**Purpose:** Actionable, prioritized alerts — the analytics engine telling you what to fix or celebrate next.

![Recommendations flow](/partner-docs/analytics/recommendations-flow.svg)

Each recommendation includes:

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Priority** | High / medium / low urgency |
| **Category** | **Opportunity** = likely problem · **Success** = positive signal · **Trend** = meaningful change vs comparison period |
| **Title & rationale** | Plain-language explanation with the numbers that triggered it |
| **Evidence link** | Deep link to Overview, Courses, Concepts, Assessments, Readiness, or Certifications |

### Example triggers

The engine compares your metrics to **thresholds you set in Analytics settings**:

- Funnel step conversion below minimum
- Completion rate below minimum (with enough volume)
- Quiz pass rate critically low
- Critical concept mastery below minimum
- Question miss rate above maximum
- Average readiness below minimum
- Certification attainment below minimum

A **minimum volume** gate prevents noisy alerts when sample size is too small (default: 10 starters).

Overview shows the top three; this tab shows the full ranked list.

## Analytics settings (in depth)

**Location:** Partner console → **Analytics settings** (not a sub-tab under Analytics, but it configures everything).

Settings are stored per ecosystem project in an **Analytics profile**. When you first open analytics, Arcademy seeds a default **Developer Education Pack** with starter concepts, a readiness model, and recommendation thresholds.

### Who can change what

| Role | View analytics | Edit terminology, sections, concepts | Edit readiness, thresholds, V2 flag, packs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Partner Analyst** | Yes | Read-only | Read-only |
| **Partner Manager** | Yes | Yes | No |
| **Partner Owner** | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Arcademy staff** | Yes | Yes | Yes (+ platform templates) |

---

### Analytics experience

**Enable Analytics V2** — Turns on the multi-tab workspace (Overview through Recommendations). When off, your team sees the classic single-page Partner Analytics Plus report.

*Owner or Platform Admin only.*

---

### Terminology & layout

Rename labels shown in analytics **without changing how numbers are calculated**:

| Setting | Default | Example customization |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Learner label** | Learner | Developer, Builder, Member |
| **Readiness label** | Learning Readiness | Integration Readiness |
| **Certification label** | Certification | Credential, Certificate |
| **Badge label** | Badge | Completion badge |

**Dashboard sections** — Toggle which analytics sections appear in the nav and Overview layout. Hidden sections stay configured but disappear from navigation.

Available section IDs include: Overview, Courses, Concepts, Assessments, Readiness, Certifications, Recommendations, Funnel, Behaviour, Cohorts, Opportunity, Trends.

**KPI set** — Advanced: metric IDs shown in custom KPI strips (one per line). Leave default unless Arcademy staff guided you. Example IDs: `completion_rate`, `quiz_pass_rate`, `health_score`.

**Funnel stages** — Advanced: event names that define funnel steps (one per line). Default stages match the standard learning journey.

*Manager+ can save terminology and layout.*

---

### Concepts

Create and manage your concept taxonomy:

- **Name** — Display label (slug auto-generated)
- **Importance** — Critical, core, or supporting
- **Description** — Optional internal note

After creating concepts, tag lessons and quiz questions in the course editor. Delete removes the concept definition (existing tags should be cleaned up in content).

*Manager+ can create and delete concepts.*

---

### Readiness model

Configure how the Readiness tab scores learners in aggregate:

| Setting | Guidance |
| --- | --- |
| **Model name** | Shown on Readiness tab |
| **Ready threshold** | Default 70 — learners at or above count as “ready” |
| **Component weights** | Must sum to 100%. Adjust to match what “ready” means for your product |

Default equal weights (25% each): course completion, quiz performance, concept mastery, required path completion.

*Owner+ only.*

---

### Certifications

- View existing certification definitions
- **Create certification** — Name + initial requirement (typically complete a specific course)
- Full publish/archive lifecycle is Owner-controlled

Certifications are intentionally separate from badges. Completing a course and earning a badge does **not** automatically grant a certification unless requirements say so.

*Manager+ can create; Owners manage lifecycle.*

---

### Recommendation thresholds

Tune when the Recommendations engine fires alerts:

| Threshold | Default | What it controls |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Funnel stage conversion min %** | 50 | Alert when a funnel step retains less than this % of the previous step |
| **Critical concept mastery min %** | 60 | Alert when critical concept mastery falls below |
| **Question miss rate max %** | 40 | Alert when a question’s miss rate exceeds |
| **Completion rate min %** | 20 | Alert when overall completion falls below |
| **Min volume for alerts** | 10 | Minimum starters before alerts activate |
| **Readiness score min** | 50 | Alert when average readiness falls below |
| **Certification attainment min %** | 10 | Alert when attainment falls below |

*Owner+ only.*

---

### Custom metric providers

Optional metric packs written for your project. The **core** provider always runs. Enable additional providers only when you understand what they add.

*Owner+ only.*

---

### Analytics packs

Install curated starter setups:

| Pack | Best for |
| --- | --- |
| **Developer Education** | Default — concepts, readiness, certifications, funnel, recommendations |
| **Documentation** | Doc-centric terminology and sections |
| **Community** | Community journey framing |
| **Hackathon** | Event/campaign-style analytics |

On install you choose:

- **Keep existing labels** — Merge pack content but preserve your terminology
- **Overwrite labels** — Replace terminology with the pack’s wording

Preview shows terminology conflicts before install.

*Owner+ only.*

## Recommended review workflow

1. **Overview** — Check health score and KPI deltas for the period
2. **Recommendations** — Work the highest-priority alerts first
3. **Courses** — Drill into any flagged course
4. **Concepts / Assessments** — Fix knowledge gaps and problematic questions
5. **Readiness / Certifications** — Track program-level outcomes
6. **Settings** — Adjust concepts, thresholds, or terminology as your program matures

## Glossary

Terms you may see in analytics, exports, or conversations with Arcademy staff.

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **KPI** | **Key Performance Indicator** — a headline number you track over time (for example, completion rate). Overview shows six default KPIs. |
| **Analytics profile** | Per-project configuration storing terminology, concepts, readiness model, thresholds, and feature flags. |
| **Analytics V2** | Multi-tab analytics workspace (this guide). Enabled per project in Settings. |
| **Partner Analytics Plus** | Classic single-page analytics report when V2 is disabled. |
| **Snapshot** | Pre-computed analytics bundle for a date range. Powers fast page loads. |
| **Period** | Selected date range for metrics (7d, 30d, 90d, all). |
| **Comparison / delta** | Percent change vs the prior matching period. |
| **Concept** | A topic or skill you define and tag on lessons/questions. |
| **Concept tag** | Link between a concept and a lesson or quiz question. |
| **Mastery** | Weighted correct-answer rate on questions tagged with a concept (0–100%). |
| **Gap score** | Urgency ranking: importance × (1 − mastery). Higher = bigger knowledge gap. |
| **Coverage** | % of lessons and questions with at least one concept tag. |
| **Importance** | Concept priority: critical (1.5×), core (1×), supporting (0.6×) in gap scoring. |
| **Funnel** | Ordered steps from page view to completion; each step’s % is events ÷ previous step. |
| **Cohort** | Group of learners by the week they first started a course. |
| **Discrimination** | How well a quiz question separates high vs low performers. |
| **Miss rate** | % of quiz attempts answered incorrectly on a question. |
| **Readiness** | Weighted score blending completion, quiz, concept, and path signals. |
| **Ready threshold** | Minimum readiness score to count as “ready” (default 70). |
| **Badge** | Automatic Arcademy credential on course completion. |
| **Certification** | Partner-defined formal credential with explicit requirements. |
| **Attainment** | Certifications awarded ÷ course starters — credential yield rate. |
| **Recommendation** | Rule-based alert with priority, category, rationale, and evidence link. |
| **Threshold** | Owner-configured limit that triggers recommendations when crossed. |
| **Min volume** | Minimum sample size before alerts fire (avoids noise on tiny data). |
| **Analytics pack** | Curated starter bundle of sections, concepts, and thresholds. |
| **Provider** | Metric calculation module (core always on; optional custom providers). |
| **Health score** | 0–100 composite of completion, quiz pass, and start conversion. |
| **Start conversion** | Course starts ÷ course page views. |
| **Executive summary** | Overview KPI strip — first thing to scan each review. |
| **Drill-down** | Detail page for one course, concept, or question. |
| **Backfill** | One-time processing of historical quiz attempts for Concepts/Assessments. |
| **Aggregate analytics** | All metrics are summarized — no individual learner PII. |
