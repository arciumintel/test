The **Partner console** is your workspace for assigned ecosystem projects after staff approval.

## Access

- URL: [Partner console](/partner-console)
- Requires a connected Solana wallet
- Available to **project admins** assigned to an ecosystem project, and to Arcademy staff

If you have no assigned projects yet, apply at [Become a partner](/partners/apply) or wait for staff to approve a pending application.

## Project picker

If you administer more than one ecosystem project, the console lists each project separately. Select a project to manage its console sections.

## Sections

### Analytics

Aggregate performance for published courses. When **Analytics V2** is enabled, you get a seven-tab workspace:

- **Overview** — KPIs, funnel, cohorts, trends, top recommendations
- **Courses** — Per-course stats and lesson/quiz drill-down
- **Concepts** — Topic mastery and knowledge gaps (requires concept tagging)
- **Assessments** — Question miss rates and quality signals
- **Readiness** — Weighted readiness score across learning signals
- **Certifications** — Partner-defined credentials (separate from badges)
- **Recommendations** — Prioritized actionable alerts

**Analytics settings** (separate nav item) controls concepts, readiness weights, certification definitions, recommendation thresholds, terminology, and the V2 experience flag.

See the full guide: [Partner analytics engine](/partners/docs/partner-console/analytics-engine).

No individual learner identities, wallet addresses, or referral attribution.

### Courses

Create, edit, and publish courses for your project. Publish readiness checks still apply (project live, lessons, quiz, badge).

### Discord

Optional integration to grant Discord roles when learners complete a course. Configure when your community workflow is ready.

### Settings

Update project metadata (including documentation and other links) and publish your project page so courses can go live.

## Recommended first visit

1. Open **Settings** — complete project details and publish the project page
2. Create and publish courses under **Courses**
3. Use **Analytics** to understand learner engagement
