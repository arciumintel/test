---
title: Roles and boundaries
description: What partners do versus what Arcademy staff does, including Partner console capabilities and limits.
---

Approved partners publish their own project pages and courses. Staff owns partner application approval and remains available to collaborate or intervene when needed.

## Partners

| You do | You do not |
| --- | --- |
| Publish your project page and courses (assigned projects only) | Manage unrelated ecosystem projects |
| Create and edit lessons, quizzes, and badges for your courses | View individual learner wallets or identities |
| Keep project links and documentation up to date in Settings | Access UTM or referral attribution dashboards |
| Refer users via stable Arcademy URLs | Bypass publish readiness checks |

## Arcademy staff

| Staff does | Staff does not |
| --- | --- |
| Approve partner applications and grant console access | Block trusted partners from publishing ready content |
| Collaborate on intake drafts when requested | Guarantee specific completion or referral numbers |
| Can review, unpublish, or quality-check published content | Share learner PII with partners |

## Partner console

After application approval, assigned project admins can use:

- **Analytics** — Funnel, trends, quiz diagnostics, and exports (aggregate only)
- **Courses** — Create, edit, publish, and unpublish courses
- **Discord** — Optional role grants for course completers
- **Settings** — Update project metadata, documentation links, and publish the project page

## Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Ecosystem project** | An Arcium-related product represented on Arcademy (use this in learner-facing context) |
| **Partner** | Your team or organization behind the ecosystem project |
| **Learner** | A user who completes courses and earns badges |
| **Badge** | Off-chain completion credential tied to a wallet |

## Explicitly excluded from partner analytics

Partner reporting does **not** include individual learner or wallet data, UTM or referrer attribution tables, or referral link click counts. Metrics are scoped to published course performance and aggregate event data only.
